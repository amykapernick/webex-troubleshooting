Connect your Audio

Connect using Computer

In the 'Quick Start' tab of your WebEx session, click 'More Options' under 'Connect Audio' and select 'Call Using Computer'. This will then prompt you to select and test your audio devices. Make sure that you can hear the sound coming through your speakers and can see the audio levels being picked up by your microphone before clicking 'OK'. Once your audio is connected, the button will turn blue and display 'Connected to Audio'. If not, click the 'Connect to Audio' button again.

Call in using a phone

In the 'Quick Start' tab of your WebEx session, click 'More Options' under 'Connect Audio' and select 'I Will Call In'. You will then be provided with a call-in number (numbers for more countries are available by selecting 'All global call-in numbers') as well as an access code (which is your meeting number) and your Attendee ID. Connect your audio by calling the number from any phone line and enter the access code and attendee ID when prompted.

Connect a Video Conferencing System

In the 'Quick Start' tab of your WebEx Session, click 'More Options' under 'Connect Audio' and select 'Call My Video System'. It will then prompt you to enter the SIP address of your video conferencing or telepresence system. If you are trying to connect your Cisco desk phone, the SIP is your Curtin email (eg. john.smith@curtin.edu.au). If you don't know the SIP of your conferencing system, select 'Call in from a video system'. This will display the SIP address of the WebEx session which can be dialed into from your video conferencing system.

Is the follwing error being displayed?

Due to low bandwith or local computer conditions (such as CPU or RAM use), video is not currently available. Video will resume automatically when conditions improve.

Low Bandwidth

WebEx has several built in features that turn on when your computer is working hard or the network is slower than required. It prioritises the different content streams, the ensure that the most important content stream (audio) is still available even when the network slows down to dial-up speeds. It will initially degrade and then drop out the video feeds (so you may notice a little lag or the video of the other end disappearing), then the content to ensure that the audio can still be heard clearly on both ends.

When sharing content by sharing your screen or sharing an application, WebEx will show a grey box over the content for all participants where the participants panel is on the host's screen. This can be moved, resized or hidden if required to show any content that it may be blocking.

To prevent this from happening, share your content by selecting to 'Share a File'. This will upload the file to WebEx rather than mirroring how it appears on your screen and prevent the content being obscured. Please note, if you are showing a PowerPoint with animations or embedded videos, this will be affected by sharing the file. Contact CITS to discuss the options for shairng animations and embedded video files in your WebEx session.

Enabling Scheduling Permission

To allow people to schedule meetings on your behalf, navigate to:

My WebEx -> Preferences -> Scheduling Options -> Scheduling Permission

Enter their email address and click Save. They can now schedule meetings for you provided they have access to your Outlook calendar.
